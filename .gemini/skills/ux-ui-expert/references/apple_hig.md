# Apple Human Interface Guidelines (HIG) Key Principles

*   **Platform**: iOS, iPadOS, macOS, watchOS, tvOS, visionOS.
*   **Core Themes**: Clarity, Deference, Depth.

## Key Principles
1.  **Clarity**: Text is legible at every size, icons are precise and lucid, adornments are subtle, and a sharpened focus on functionality motivates the design.
2.  **Deference**: Fluid motion and a crisp, beautiful interface help people understand and interact with content without competing with it. Content fills the entire screen.
3.  **Depth**: Visual layers and realistic motion convey hierarchy, impart vitality, and facilitate understanding.

## Typography
*   System Font: San Francisco (SF Pro for iOS/macOS).
*   **Dynamic Type**: Automatically adjusts letter spacing and line height based on size, and allows users to set preferred reading sizes across the OS.

## Color
*   **Semantic Colors**: Use colors like `.systemBlue` instead of hardcoded hex values to automatically adapt to Light/Dark mode and accessibility settings.
*   **Vibrancy & Materials**: Use blurring and translucency (materials) to hint at content behind the active view, establishing depth.

## Navigation & Layout
*   **Tab Bar**: Bottom navigation, distinct from toolbars.
*   **Navigation Bar**: Top bar, usually with a back button leading to a previous screen (hierarchical navigation).
*   **Modals & Sheets**: Use for self-contained tasks. Sheets slide up from the bottom; full-screen modals take over the view.

## Interaction
*   **Gestures**: Swipe to go back, pull to refresh, pinch to zoom. Interfaces should feel physical and respond to natural gestures.
*   **Haptics**: Subtle vibrations provide physical feedback for actions (e.g., successful payment, switching a toggle).
